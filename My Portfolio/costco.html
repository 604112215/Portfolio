COSTCO SHARING

Costco Sharing is a new service designed for the people who are single but concern about buying a huge amount of items at Costco because they are single. They cannot afford or finish such amount of products on time. This problem can be solved by Costco Sharing, which will attract tons of new customers to be the Costco Member. This service is suitable for both customers and Costco itself.

APPROACH

As a team, we have applied various research methods to get the final insights. For example, we did Service Safari to experience how people are shopping at Costco and discover some problems. We also used the Contextual Inquiry to see users’ shortcut of making a decision.

MY ROLE

Everyone on our team played essential roles. We allocated the tasks to maximize our work progress. I’ve worked on collecting data in the research process and building physical environment prototype (shopping cart, shelf, locker, etc.) by using foam core, designed a screen clipper by using 3D printers and leading an origami practice to come up with initial insights.

DESIGN PROCESS

SERVICE SAFARI

We went to Costco and experienced how to shopping there and what kind of services that Costco provides to its customers.

BUSINESS ORIGAMI (Part A): PROBLEMS

To find the problems and pain points that users are facing, we did a business origami after finishing the service safari. We played different roles with different concerns and in different simulative situations, with the purpose to understand what kind of decisions they would make and experience what they may encounter.

PROBLEM STATEMENT

We found a problem when doing Business Origami that young people who are single are easy to give up buying some stuff when they are shopping at Costco. Thus we made a contextual inquiry about what kind of factors will influence the decision making of buying things at Costco.  This research helped us receive some factors affecting the decision making of young people.

KEY FACTORS OF MAKING DECISION AT COSTCO

We discovered five key factors that influence users' decision making when they are shopping at Costco. People may give up buying something when they concern those factors.

BUSINESS ORIGAMI (Part B) - SOLUTION: SHARING SERVICE

After defining the problems that users are facing, we did the business origami again to figure out a possible solution to solve these problems. In this part, we come up with an extra service called Share Service into users’ shopping journey. If a user thinks the volume of a kind of goods is too big, he/she can share the rest of the product with other users. Even this solution is a bold and rough one; it reveals users’ mental model of sharing a product at Costco and shortcut to solve the problems of this kind. Indeed, in the processes of doing the business origami to perform different situations, we did find many side-effects caused by this new service.

PROBLEMS

Calculating sharing activity is always a hard thing. There are a lot of potential problems if we want to make this service come true.

1. If people can only buy the number of things that they need, it will damage the business model of Costco. The business model of Costco is to sell a significant amount of products. In this way, Costco can earn the substantial profit from it. In this sense, if it starts to offer sharing service, it means Costco may not sell out the same volume as the past and customers will only buy the portions of the products based on their own need.

2. If this service becomes true, Costco will face problems of storage and pipeline problem. There are some possible situations, as shown followed.

INSIGHT​S

Upgrade Membership Card

Customers can upgrade the membership card for the sharing service.  
Pros:
It‘ll help to divide the customers who need a sharing service from others. 
It can relieve the problems of waiting line and storage caused by this sharing service. 
It’ll bring Costco extra profit.

Supporting Screen

The screen can be attached on Costco shopping cart. 
It will show a customer the shared items around her/him based on shelves nearby when she/he are shopping. 
The customer can add shared items on the screen.

Fixed Portion

Customers only can choose the fixed portion of shared items.  
In this case, the storage and back-side stage problem for Costco can be solved, and this service can be realized.

I led my team members to build a physical prototype using foam core. We made three significant elements that can help us define the scenario of Costco Sharing: Pick up and check-in area, Supporting screen and Shelves. The physical prototype helps us bring our story and scenario to users.

DIGITAL PROTOTYPE

The challenge of prototyping is how to build a simple interaction of choosing pick-up items and shared items. Should those items be checked into the same locker or not?

KEY LEARNING

1. Think question chronologically. Service Design is a large construction. When doing service design, We thought over questions in different positions. How individuals interact with the organization? How is every single interaction related to other parts of the organization?

2. Think about side-effects. Each solution may cause some side-effect when it is come up with. Thus, we not only focused on the solution itself but also focused on how to deal with the side-effects created by the solution and try to come up with a better assumption.
